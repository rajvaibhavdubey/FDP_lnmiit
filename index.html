Workshops - Project Sessions - Industry Leaders

The Government of Rajasthan and the Lakshmi & Usha Mittal (LUM) Foundation jointly established The LNM Institute of Information Technology (The LNMIIT) in 2002 as a Public-Private-Partnership (PPP) venture, under the Chairmanship of the well-known industrialist and philanthropist, Padma Vibhushan Mr. Lakshmi N. Mittal. This is a not-for-profit institute and it solely depends on the resource generation from student tuition fees. The LNMIIT is a fully residential campus for the students, and partly residential for faculty and a few emergency non-teaching staff. It is spread over 100-acres of land on the outskirts of Jaipur city of Rajasthan, the pink city of India (about 10 km away from Jaipur-Agra National Highway). The institute started its academic operations, as an autonomous institute, in 2003 with a batch of 29 students and was granted the 'Deemed-to- be University' status in 2006 under section - 3 of UGC Act 1956 in 'de-novo' category. In addition to having been accredited by the National Assessment & Accreditation Council (NAAC) as an ‘A’ grade institution, all the engineering programmes of the institute are approved by the AICTE. The campus presently houses around 2000 undergraduate and about 100 postgraduate students. Although the focus areas of teaching and research at the institute are in the broad field of Information Technology (IT), it has expanded its academic horizons with the changing industrial and economic focus at the state and national level. At present The LNMIIT has six departments - Departments of CSE, ECE, MME, Maths, Physics and HSS. All Engineering departments offer UG, PG and PhD programmes. And all science departments offer PG and PhD programmes.

Meet the Right People

The objective of this event is to introduce the fundamentals of blockchain technology and the cryptographic primitives/protocol used for Bit-coin as well as implementation of blockchain with real time applications. This features high level discussion of Nakamoto's Bit-coin protocol, Bitcoin mining, cryptocurrency, Blockchain programming, Solidity, Ethereum, the ideas of distributed ledger, Hyperledger, MultiChain, privacy and permission in multichain, mining in multichain. Overall, this FDP serves to be a great platform for faculty and researchers to upgrade their knowledge in Blockchain technology.
